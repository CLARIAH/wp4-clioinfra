	Country

8. Keywords (5)

	Height, Anthropometrics, Living Standard.Demographics, Infant Mortality


9. Abstract (200 words)

	Infant mortality was large component of overall mortailtiy, because
children in their first year of life are particularly vulnerable.
Moreover, the variable can serve as an indicator of the disease
environment, when it comes to assessing the proximate determinants of
height.

10. Time period

	1810 -2000. All data refer to the decadal average (1810 means 1810-19
etc)

11. Geographical coverage

	Worldwide

12. Methodologies used for data collection and processing

	Reconstruction of infant mortality by decade using a variety of
different sources, but mainly the data set of Aboubarb, M.R.  and
Kimball, A.L. (2007). See also the text below.

13. Data quality

	As good as possible, but counter-checking and improvement welcome.

14. Date of collection

	See references

15. Data collectors

	Joerg Baten, Mathias Blum, and many colleagues from around the world.

17. Text

This is an excerpt of the paper by Baten and Blum (2012), who included
infant mortality rates in regressions of height order to control for
disease environment. The results confirmed their expectations: a
problematic disease environment is associated with lower heights, the
standard-deviation effect being about twice the size of the protein
effect.
